The project

The idea of the project started during recent trips to our homelands. While traveling through a culturally rich part of India where the great king Maharana Pratap Singh once lived, Abhishek trying to gather more insights into his life realized that the king is still popular now and there are myriad people unknown to the world, who helped the king preserve the culture and homeland from the invaders are forgotten. In many ways how a digital tool being the king and its creators being the myriad of people helps not only create but preserve culture but are forgotten. This became the seed for our work “Maharana”.

Maharana is an audio-visual artwork that revolves around the creation and destruction of a planet. The work starts with numerous gaseous particle clouds of different colors resembling the nascent stage of a planet formation. From these gaseous mixtures, numerous polygons and figures of varying sizes and colors start forming representing the conversion of gas to solid matter which comes together to form a planet. In this system, we intend to represent the underlying principles on which a tool is created by the clouds of particles. On these principles (clouds) when different creators(polygons) come together a digital tool (planet) starts shaping up. This tool is then improved by the contribution of community members (meteorites). As we start removing the effort of these creators and inputs from the community members the planet starts to die, metaphorically showing how the sustenance of the tools depends on the contribution of this myriad of people unknown to the world. This visual representation will be coupled with audio. Moreover, we intend to gather actual data on some open-source tools, and the creators and contributors who sustain these tools.

About Us

Rajabegam

  • Rajabegam is a new project by Barcelona-based Sofia Vallejo and Stockholm-based Abhishek Choubey. This project stems from their deep love for electronic media and audio-visual art. Sofia and Abhishek’s artistic vision for Rajabegam is to create multi-sensory media that provokes knowing and thinking about cultural exploration and tech innovation. Their process often involves working with collections of information or data to draw narratives showcasing cultures across the world through immersive audio-visual art. The creative process is infused with exploration and enjoyment, driven by the underlying principle of using art to reflect the Unknowns. The unknown aspects of fading cultures, forgotten communities, or the pursuit and expansion of unknown boundaries. Rajabegam emerges as an opportunity for Sofia and Abhishek to enrich both their own and others' unknown aesthetic experiences.

    • Abhishek Choubey

  • Abhishek, hailing from India, embarked on his academic journey with a bachelor's degree in Electronics and Instrumentation Engineering from Shri Govindram Seksaria Institute of Technology and Science, Indore. Intrigued by electronic music, its technology, and the Indian music industry, he delved into the field as an artist manager and DJ post-graduation. Realizing a strong inclination towards technology, he pursued his first master's degree in Music Communication and Technology at the Norwegian University of Science and Technology, Trondheim, Norway. This experience provided a holistic perspective on music technology, sparking a keen interest in audio signal processing, machine learning, and music information retrieval. Seeking further expertise, Abhishek pursued his second master's with the Music Technology Group at Pompeu Fabra University in Barcelona, Spain. Presently, he is undertaking a Ph.D. at KTH Royal Institute of Technology, Stockholm, Sweden, within the Lullabyte Doctoral network. His research focuses on exploring the impact of sounds and music on sleep, with the aim of developing personalized sonic interaction design tools to promote healthy sleeping habits.
